# Design Archetypes for Presentation Decks

A curated collection of 8 visual identities inspired by global research firms, consulting groups, and premier design agencies. Each archetype pairs a specific palette, typography approach, layout philosophy, and signature element. Use these as a springboard when choosing the visual direction for a presentation—either adopt one wholesale or thoughtfully blend two that best fit your brief.

---

## RESEARCH-GROUP ARCHETYPES

### 1. Global Insight (McKinsey-style)

**Inspiration:** McKinsey's editorial research publications and digital reports—known for authoritative, data-driven presentations to executive audiences.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Navy Primary | #1A2B4C | 60-70% of slide backgrounds, title bars, dominant elements | Deep, authoritative, professional |
| White Base | #FFFFFF | Text, content area backgrounds, breathing room | Ensures legibility and clean hierarchy |
| Cobalt Accent | #2E5BFF | Headlines, key metrics, callouts, visual emphasis | Bright enough to draw attention without harshness |
| Light Gray | #F5F5F5 | Supporting backgrounds, section dividers, subtle texture | Reduces contrast fatigue on content slides |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard Bold (44pt titles, 20pt section headers) — 깔끔하고 전문적
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14-16pt) — 명확하고 읽기 편함
- **Data/Stats | 통계**: Large Pretendard Bold numerals (60-72pt) paired with Noto Sans KR Medium labels (12pt) — creates visual hierarchy for numbers | 숫자 계층 구조 강화

**Layout Patterns**
- Strict left-aligned grid with ample white space
- Thin horizontal rules separating headline from body content
- Every chart framed as an "Exhibit" with title above and source line below (e.g., "Exhibit 1: Global AI Market Growth")
- Single-column narrative or two-column with visual on right

**Signature Element**
Large oversized stat callouts (60-72pt navy or cobalt number, 12-14pt label below in gray). These appear on data-heavy slides and immediately signal "this is important data." Pair with a thin horizontal rule above or below to frame the number.

**Best For**
- Executive market analysis and strategy decks
- Data-heavy presentations to C-level audiences
- Consulting firm recommendations or research reports
- Industry analyses or competitive landscapes

**Usage Example**
A deck on "AI Market Trends for 2026" would use the navy/cobalt palette, left-aligned grid layouts, traditional sans-serif type, and stat callouts to convey rigor and authoritative insight.

### 2. Structured Argument (BCG-style)

**Inspiration:** Boston Consulting Group's famous "pyramid principle" decks—every slide is a complete thought, hierarchically structured.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Charcoal | #2D2D2D | Primary text, titles, strong visual anchors | Professional, non-blue alternative to navy |
| Forest Green | #1B4332 | Accent color for key claims, section headers, visual emphasis | Warm, grounded, suggests stability |
| Warm Gray | #F2F1ED | Content area backgrounds, slide bases, subtle texture | Avoids harsh white; feels editorial |
| Cream | #FEFDFB | Text backgrounds, callout boxes, high-contrast readability | Pairs with charcoal for maximum contrast |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard Bold (44pt titles, 20pt section headers) — 클래식하면서도 현대적
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14-16pt) — 읽기 편한 본문 텍스트
- **Emphasis | 강조**: Pretendard Bold in Forest Green for key claims or governing thoughts

**Layout Patterns**
- "Governing Thought" banner at the top of each content slide (full-sentence claim, not a topic label)
- 2-3 column supporting evidence below the governing thought
- Numbered lists or pyramid structure to show logical hierarchy
- Section dividers with forest green background and white text

**Signature Element**
The "Governing Thought"—a full, bolded sentence at the top of each slide stating the slide's core claim or takeaway. This is not a title; it's an argument. Example: "The market is expanding faster than analysts predicted, driven by unexpected enterprise adoption."

**Best For**
- Argument-driven recommendation decks
- Board presentations and strategic updates
- Internal consulting deliverables
- Situation/Complication/Resolution narratives

**Usage Example**
A deck on "Strategic Response to Market Disruption" would use serif headers to evoke analytical rigor, each slide opening with a complete-sentence claim, and forest-green accents to tie sections together.

### 3. Bold Minimal (Bain-style)

**Inspiration:** Bain & Company's punchy, high-contrast visual language—favors boldness, simplicity, and visual surprise.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bright Red | #CC0000 | Dominant accent, high-impact elements, call-to-action text | Signal for attention; use sparingly for maximum effect |
| Black | #000000 | Primary text, strong backgrounds, maximum contrast | Ensures legibility and dramatic impact |
| White | #FFFFFF | Text on colored backgrounds, breathing room, content areas | Clean, uncluttered, professional |
| Dark Gray | #4A4A4A | Supporting text, secondary information, fine print | Prevents visual monotony of pure black |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard ExtraBold (48pt titles, 24pt section headers) — 강렬하고 주목성 높음
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14pt) — 굵은 제목과의 대비 강조
- **Accent | 강조**: Pretendard Bold in Bright Red for key phrases or calls-to-action

**Layout Patterns**
- Large blocks of solid red or black with white text (full-bleed color on sections)
- Minimal content per slide—one idea, one visual element, one stat
- Generous negative space; whitespace as a design tool
- Asymmetric layouts with bold shapes (large rectangles, bands of color)

**Signature Element**
Single bold geometric shape (rectangle, band of color, or solid block) anchoring each slide's key message. The shape contains the headline or stat in bold white or black text. Minimal decoration; maximum visual impact.

**Best For**
- Executive summaries requiring instant understanding
- Kickoff presentations or results reveals
- High-stakes board updates
- Pitches requiring confidence and decisiveness

**Usage Example**
A deck on "Q3 Results & Accelerated Growth" would feature a red banner across the top with white bold text stating the key message, simple charts with red accent lines, and lots of white space to emphasize each data point.

### 4. Future Signals (Gartner/Deloitte-style tech-forward research)

**Inspiration:** Gartner's Hype Cycle reports and Deloitte's technology trend research—designed to communicate innovation, maturity models, and future readiness.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deep Indigo | #19376D | Primary backgrounds, authoritative foundation | Modern, tech-forward, sophisticated |
| Electric Cyan | #00B4D8 | Accent color, highlights, emphasis, data visualization | Signals technology and innovation |
| Light Gray | #F0F0F0 | Content area backgrounds, contrast areas | Ensures readability on dark base |
| Charcoal | #2D2D2D | Secondary text, supporting elements | Pairs with indigo and cyan |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard Bold (44pt titles, 20pt section headers) — 기하학적, 현대적 느낌
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14-16pt) — 중립적이고 기술적 분위기
- **Data | 데이터**: Noto Sans KR Medium for labels, metrics, source attribution (12-14pt) — tech-forward look

**Layout Patterns**
- Quadrant and matrix layouts (2x2 frameworks showing maturity, positioning, or scenarios)
- Maturity curve or "hype cycle" style visuals with indigo background and cyan-highlighted inflection points
- Timeline or journey maps showing progression or phases
- Network diagrams with nodes connected by thin lines (ecosystem motif)

**Signature Element**
Small circular "node" icons (diameter ~20-30px) connected by thin cyan lines, repeated as section dividers, accent elements, or visual metaphors for ecosystems. Often used to signal "these elements are connected" or "this is a network."

**Best For**
- Technology trend and market-maturity presentations
- Innovation or digital transformation decks
- Analyst research or landscape reports
- Strategic technology roadmaps or capability matrices

**Usage Example**
A deck on "AI Adoption Maturity and Market Positioning" would use the indigo/cyan palette, a 2x2 maturity matrix with companies positioned by adoption vs. capability, connected node icons to show competitive relationships, and monospace labels for precise tech specifications.

## AD-AGENCY ARCHETYPES

### 5. Systemic Identity (Pentagram-style)

**Inspiration:** Pentagram's brand identity work—known for creating distinctive visual systems where every element reinforces a unique mark or identity.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black | #000000 | Primary text, strong backgrounds, structure | Classic, timeless, maximum contrast |
| White | #FFFFFF | Text, breathing room, content areas | Clean and sophisticated |
| Saturated Accent | #F4FF00 (or #FF3CAC) | Unexpected, bold accent color; used sparingly | Acid yellow OR hot magenta—one unexpected choice |
| Light Gray | #F5F5F5 | Secondary backgrounds, supporting elements | Reduces pure black/white monotony |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard ExtraBold in large scale (52-60pt), sometimes cropped or bleeding off the edge — 기억에 남는 효과
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium at standard scale (14-16pt) — 제목 강조 극대화
- **Accent | 강조**: Pretendard Bold in different weight for emphasis

**Layout Patterns**
- Asymmetric grid; elements don't follow strict alignment
- Generous bleed (content running to the edge of the slide)
- Large flat color fields as backgrounds for section dividers
- Hierarchical use of white space to separate sections

**Signature Element**
A custom mark, monogram, or geometric shape unique to the presentation (designed for this brief). This mark appears as a subtle watermark or corner element on every slide, tying the entire deck together visually. The mark should feel intentional and memorable—not decorative.

**Best For**
- Brand-identity or rebrand pitch decks
- Creative agency capability presentations
- Design-focused or design-thinking presentations
- Luxury or premium brand presentations

**Usage Example**
A creative agency pitching a brand rebrand might feature acid-yellow accents, a custom geometric mark in the corner of every slide, oversized chapter headings in a distinctive display font, and asymmetric layouts that feel intentional rather than accidental.

### 6. Conversational Provocateur (Wieden+Kennedy-style)

**Inspiration:** Wieden+Kennedy's famous "Just Do It" campaign and their deck aesthetic—bold, brief, conversational, text-as-image.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warm Off-White | #F4F1EA | Primary slide background, breathing room | Approachable, warm, not sterile |
| Punchy Orange | #FF5A1F | Accent color, highlights, calls-to-action | Energetic, confident, memorable |
| Electric Blue | #0055CC | Secondary accent, contrast, visual variety | Adds tension with orange; creates vibrancy |
| Full-Bleed Black | #000000 | Occasional dark slides, text emphasis, dramatic moments | Breaks up the warm palette with punch |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard Bold Condensed (50-60pt) — 짧고 임팩 있는 문구 표현
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium at comfortable reading size (14-16pt)
- **Accent | 강조**: Hand-written or informal style effect using Pretendard sparingly for personal touch

**Layout Patterns**
- Text-as-image: single large statements occupying full slides (e.g., "The future is not just about technology. It's about culture.")
- Image-heavy storytelling slides with minimal text overlay
- Full-bleed color slides breaking up narrative flow
- Manifesto-style slides interspersed between visual content

**Signature Element**
Short, copy-driven "manifesto" slides appearing every 3-4 slides. These slides feature a single bold statement (1 sentence, 10-15 words max) in large orange or blue text on the warm background, no other content. They function as memorable anchors and breaks in the narrative.

**Best For**
- Creative campaign pitches to marketing teams
- Brand strategy decks emphasizing culture and positioning
- Startup pitches with bold vision statements
- Internal culture or values presentations

**Usage Example**
A creative campaign pitch on "Redefining Customer Connection" might feature full-bleed orange slides with white text stating bold claims, high-impact photography on image slides, and interspersed manifesto statements like "We believe advertising should make people feel, not interrupt them."

### 7. Classic Craft (Ogilvy-style)

**Inspiration:** Ogilvy's heritage in print advertising and brand building—emphasizing craftsmanship, case studies, and proven results.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parchment | #FAF6EE | Primary background, warm and inviting | Evokes print heritage; supports readability |
| Deep Red | #A6192E | Headlines, accent, emphasis, authority | Classic, dignified, trustworthy (Ogilvy signature) |
| Navy | #001F3F | Secondary accent, supporting text, balance | Adds depth and formality |
| Warm Gray | #8B8680 | Supporting text, captions, subtle details | Sophisticated, not harsh |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard Bold (36-44pt) — 클래식하면서도 우아한 인쇄 전통 표현
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14-16pt) — 따뜻하면서도 명확한 본문
- **Captions | 캡션**: Noto Sans KR Medium at 10-12pt in warm gray — editorial style | 에디토리얼 스타일

**Layout Patterns**
- Magazine/editorial-style layouts with pull quotes highlighted in red
- Case-study callout boxes with thin borders and small wordmark/logo placeholders
- Image-text pairings with byline-style attribution ("By [Client Name]" or "Case Study: [Company]")
- Numbered case studies (1, 2, 3) creating a narrative of successes
- Generous margins and white space reflecting print magazine design

**Signature Element**
Framed case-study callout boxes—a thin red border around a 2-3 sentence case summary, with a placeholder for a client logo or icon. These boxes appear consistently and signal "real client, real results."

**Best For**
- Brand heritage and evolution presentations
- Case-study-driven or client-results decks
- Advertising agency portfolios or capabilities
- Heritage brands or organizations with established reputations

**Usage Example**
An advertising agency presenting "20 Years of Award-Winning Campaigns" would use classic serif headers in deep red, magazine-style layouts with pull quotes, case-study boxes for each major campaign, and generous white space emphasizing the craftsmanship and history.

### 8. Connected Systems (R/GA · IDEO-style)

**Inspiration:** R/GA and IDEO's human-centered design and innovation presentations—playful, optimistic, systems-thinking, and approachable.

**Color Palette**
| Color Name | Hex | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vibrant Coral | #FF6B6B | Primary accent (60% visual weight), headlines, emphasis | Warm, optimistic, human-centered |
| Teal | #4ECDC4 | Secondary accent, supporting elements, data highlights | Complements coral; feels connected, sustainable |
| Sunny Yellow | #FFE66D | Tertiary accent, callouts, light emphasis | Adds vibrancy; used sparingly |
| White | #FFFFFF | Base/background, text, breathing room | Keeps the palette playful but readable |

**Typography | 타이포그래피**
- **Headers | 제목**: Pretendard SemiBold (44-52pt) — 친근하고 접근 가능, 인간 중심적
- **Body | 본문**: Noto Sans KR Medium (14-16pt) — 읽기 쉽고 현대적, 따뜻한 느낌
- **Accent | 강조**: Hand-drawn or sketch-style annotations using Pretendard Light for playful feel

**Layout Patterns**
- Sticky-note / whiteboard-inspired grids of content blocks
- Journey maps showing user flows or process stages
- Process-flow diagrams with numbered steps and connecting arrows
- Icon + label combinations (small icons next to short descriptions, repeated in rows)
- Lots of white space between blocks to feel exploratory and open

**Signature Element**
Hand-drawn arrows, circles, and check-mark annotations (in coral or teal) highlighting key data points or annotating visuals. These feel like workshop artifacts—as if someone sketched notes directly onto the research insights. This signals "human-driven," "collaborative," and "iterative thinking."

**Best For**
- Human-centered design or innovation presentations
- Digital transformation or change-management decks
- Workshop summaries or collaborative project outcomes
- Internal team updates on process improvements or new initiatives
- Startup pitches emphasizing user empathy

**Usage Example**
A UX design team presenting "Redesigning the Customer Journey" would use rounded-sans headers in coral, whiteboard-style grids showing user personas and journey stages, connecting arrows between stages, and hand-drawn annotation marks highlighting key pain points or opportunities.

## How to Use This Reference

1. **Read your design brief** (from data-organizer-agent's design-brief.md)
2. **Scan the archetypes** above and identify 1-2 that resonate with your topic/audience
3. **Note the key elements**: palette, typography, layout philosophy, signature element
4. **Adapt or blend**: You can adopt one archetype wholesale, or thoughtfully combine two (e.g., "Structured Argument palette with Future Signals layout philosophy")
5. **Document your choice** in design-brief.md or a separate design-system.md with the exact colors, fonts, and layout rules you'll hand off to the pptx skill
